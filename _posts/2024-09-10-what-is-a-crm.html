Streamline Customer Relationships, Enhance Sales, and Drive Growth with the Right CRM

History

From humble beginnings in the Rolodexes of the 1980s and spreadsheets of the 1990s and 2000’s through to the advanced automation, AI and analytics of modern CRM systems, CRM systems have always been important for businesses interested in growing, managing and retaining their customer base.

Despite CRM software having been around for decades, not all businesses make use of one. Instead, many often keep vital information, like customer details, stored across scattered spreadsheets, wallets full of business cards, and scraps of paper - these inefficient methods are incredibly difficult to share with a team. If this is you (or you’re just interested in implementing a CRM) then read on!

Understanding CRM Systems

At its heart, a CRM system is a piece of software used to manage your business's customer relationships. From simple-to-use full spectrum solutions like HubSpot to dedicated sales & marketing solutions like Pipedrive, through to enterprise software solutions like Salesforce and Microsoft Dynamics 365, there is a CRM solution for every type of business.

What a CRM system isn’t is a silver bullet. Implementing one will not magically get you sales, nor double your contract sizes. But what it will do is help your company to streamline your business processes, align departments such as sales, marketing and customer service and allow you to be systematic in your approach towards reaching out to prospective clients, which in turn helps you make more outreach and convert more sales with the help of tools like automations.

Key Features of a CRM System

Modern CRM systems offer a broad range features, but some common components you can expect to be included are:

  • Lead Management: Potential customers can be added to the system as leads. You can differentiate between types of leads to evaluate where your customers are coming from.
  • Sales Pipelines: Allows you to define (and understand) the stages that your customers go through before buying from you and gives you a visual oversight of your entire sales process.
  • Reports and Forecasting: As you add more data to your CRM, reports will help you understand your business and identify trends, allowing you to focus on the right types of customers. Forecasting helps you determine where your business will realistically be in the future.
  • Automation: Business process automations can help speed up processes, such as email outreach, saving your organisation time and enabling you to scale your business as fewer hands are required on deck to achieve the same results.
  • Invoicing and proposals: Create, draft and send Invoices and proposals to your clients and prospects.

Depending on your needs you can look to consolidate all of this information in one centralised location, instead of building a large tech stack to gain the same functionality, or you can look at separate systems, in a “best in class” type scenario and add features as you require them, just ensure that these applications can talk to one another. This can easily be done with automation systems like Zapier where you can build automated workflows between over 7000 different apps.

Why Does Your Business Need a CRM?

Managing your customer relationships via spreadsheet is no longer sufficient and there are many reasons why you’d want to implement a CRM, some of which we’ve listed below:

  • You’re looking to centralise your customer information - This ensures that your entire team has access to up-to-date and accurate information about your clients.
  • You want to fortify your customer relationships - You can track every interaction a customer has with your business, allowing you to understand their needs and preferences better. This information ultimately helps you to tailor your communications and offers, leading to stronger, more personal relationships with customers.
  • You want to improve your sales - A CRM provides your organisation with the tools needed to manage and streamline sales processes. You can easily scale your sales by increasing outreach without necessarily adding and ensuring that no potential sale falls through the cracks. If a sales person leaves your organisation it is no longer the death knell for interactions with the customers they have converted.
  • You want to boost productivity - Routine tasks such as follow-up emails, meeting scheduling, and data entry can be automated. This frees up your team, no matter the size, to be able to focus on what’s important. Company wide email templates help ensure consistency in your sales and marketing efforts and are available across your teams.
  • You want to gain actionable insights into your customers - Modern CRMs come equipped with powerful analytics and reporting tools. By analyzing data from your CRM, you can gain valuable insights into your sales performance, customer behavior, and marketing effectiveness. These insights enable you to make informed decisions, identify areas for improvement, and refine your business strategies.
  • Enhance Collaboration Across Teams - A CRM fosters collaboration by providing a single platform where different departments can access and update customer information. Whether it's sales, marketing, or customer service, everyone can work together seamlessly, ensuring a consistent and coordinated approach to customer engagement.
  • Support Scalability - As your business grows, so does the complexity of managing customer relationships. A CRM system is designed to scale with your business. It can handle an increasing volume of data, support more users, and integrate with other tools and systems you might adopt in the future.

Implementing a CRM system is a strategic investment that can significantly impact your business's growth and success. By centralising customer information, enhancing relationships, improving sales management, and providing valuable insights, a CRM positions your business for sustainable growth.

When should you start using a CRM system?

It’s never too early to start using a CRM system in your business. If your aim is to identify and contact multiple potential clients, or manage customer service interactions, setting up a CRM system can provide you with all the necessary tools right from the start.

Top CRM Tips

Implement a CRM Early for Centralised Customer Data Management - Prevent data silos, enhance team collaboration and make it easier for your business to to manage customer relationships from day 1.

Automate Routine Tasks to Boost Productivity - If you automate early, you can scale your sales outreach efficiently. Automate follow up emails, follow up tasks and data entry for new prospects to save you time and focus on high priority tasks that grow your business.

Leverage CRM Insights to Drive Sales and Improve Strategy - Ensure analytics are set up so you can quickly identify trends, for example, open rates, marketing effectiveness and sales performance and are able to adjust your processes accordingly.
